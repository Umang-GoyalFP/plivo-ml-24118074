Why this configuration

Under a fixed 2,000-step cap, the only two levers that increase effective training signal rather than compete for a fixed share of it are: more real content per token (BPE tokenizer shrinks Devanagari sequences, so each block_size window covers more actual text) and more tokens per step (bigger batch raises steps × batch = total tokens seen, with lower gradient noise). Every other change tried — weight tying, scaled init, RoPE, larger block_size — is a longer-horizon bet that needs more than 2,000 steps to recover its own instability cost, so each lost to the simpler baseline choice at this step count.

Why the failed runs failed

  • ckpt2 (first schedule attempt): ran with default args instead of tuned ones. Cosine decay from an unraised peak lr lowers the average lr across training below the constant baseline; weight_decay 0.1 is calibrated for training runs far longer than 2000 steps and dragged weights toward zero faster than gradients could shape them.
  • Weight tying (ckpt3, ckpt3a): tying head and tok_emb forces one matrix to serve two conflicting roles — clustering similar inputs vs. separating outputs for confident logits. 2000 steps isn't enough to resolve that tension.
  • Scaled init (ckpt3, ckpt3b): depth-scaled/GPT-2-style init is designed for much longer training; it's undertrained relative to the flat std=0.05 baseline at this step count.
  • Larger block_size (ckpt4): more pos_emb params and compute per step, but the corpus/step budget is too short to actually learn to exploit the extra context — wasted capacity, plus 2x slower steps.
  • RoPE (ckpt5): has no learned parameters — the model must learn relative-position use implicitly through Q/K dot products, which converges slower than a directly learnable pos_emb table. RoPE's real advantage (long-range generalization) never gets time to pay off in 2000 steps at block_size 128.

Why this order of experiments

Architecture/optimizer/init/tying/RoPE/block_size changes were tested first because they're cheap (~75–165ms/step) to try and discard. Batch size and vocab_size increases were saved for last and applied once, on top of the already-best config, because both raise ms/step for every run from that point forward — testing them early would have taxed the time budget of every subsequent experiment. Both were fairly high-confidence wins going in, which is also why they were treated as the closing moves rather than early exploration.
